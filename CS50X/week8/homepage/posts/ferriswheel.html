Ferris wheel of friends

8 July 2018 by Rais

This was a story of our first date.

It was a beautiful moment, but one of mixed feelings. Looking out our pod, I see a breathtaking view of the 1950-esque theme park, glittering with shimmering lights and joyous faces. As we reach the top of the ferris wheel, we're privileged with a brief but scenic view of Sydney's iconic coastal skyline, the Harbour Bridge and Opera House framed in a perfect shot.

She sits beside me, recollecting our day - ice skating, hot chocolates and a heaping load of banter. Gesturing at the changing colourful lights of the ferris wheel, she tells me how beautiful she thinks this ride is, not knowing that I was fixated on the two clowns below us, looming around on stilts, now ironically looking like little ants.

I turn to face her, looking at her curiously, trying to remember what was it she just told me. I hold her gaze for a moment and smile "Very beautiful". She was.

The ferris wheel makes a sudden stop, to allow more passengers into the other pods. We're both now suspended in mid-air, alone and in close proximity. I slowly lean away from her as I notice how strange this feels. This was not supposed to be a date. We joked all day about how she had turned down many guys who started off as friends but eventually developed feelings for her. I didn't want to be one of those guys, but I was.

I scramble to make a joke, because jokes just make everything better.

"Hey, wouldn't it be funny if I put my arm around you now, and tussle your hair?" making a reference to a story she had told me today, where her friend did just that as an awkward attempt to flirt with her. She turned him down soon after.

"Are you going to tell me we need to stop hanging out?" I say with sly confidence, despite my hand freezing in hesitation right before her shoulder. This is probably not the best joke to make. I let out a chuckle but racing through my mind are nervous thoughts of whether actually placing my arm around her will lead to me receiving the same treatment as the other guy. I decided not to find out so I lean my arm on the seat rest behind her, a safe and respectful distance, in the friend zone.

I truly did enjoy her company today. We are great as friends, and that is something I am not prepared to risk. When I think about it, all I really want is to spend more time with her and have good conversations. Friends can do that.

She tells me this ride would have been romantic if she could have done it with a boyfriend. "Yeah, it is a romantic ride. Maybe next time" I say.

The ferris wheel slows before coming to another halt and the conductor opens the door to our pod.

She walks out, and I follow after her from behind, as a good friend and nothing more.
